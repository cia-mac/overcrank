What you're looking at

Six cameras from the current 1920 × 1080 @ 1,000 fps query. Same data, new primitive. Cards compressed from ~400 px tall each to 92 px — roughly 4× density.

  • Thumbnail slot on the left is placeholder for a real product image. The monogram over gradient tint says "this is where brand recognition happens."
  • Headroom chip keeps its green/yellow/amber coloring and is the first thing the eye lands on after the image.
  • Brand + Model + Category stacked, same rhythm as cards.
  • Best match gets its own column now — the thing the user came here to see.
  • Three spec columns (sensor / bit / tier) replace the meta row from cards. Identical info, 1/3 the vertical space.
  • Compare button on the right, same size (32 px) and same visual language as before.
  • Row click would open detail inline (expand the row) or in a right-side drawer. No modal.

What's missing for this to ship: thumbnail images. Without them the left slot is decorative. With them, this view replaces cards.
